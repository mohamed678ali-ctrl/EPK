Biography

MXZY makes music that hits you first thing in the morning and stays with you long after. Equal parts complex and immediate — the kind of record that makes you sit with it, think through it, and come back. Some tracks will get you in your bag. Some will get you lit. That’s intentional.

Born Somali-Canadian and raised in Ottawa, MXZY (Mohamed Ali) moves like the culture he grew up in: old school hoops energy, vintage store patience, soul food warmth. He was writing the kind of hip-hop that makes you think — dense, layered, technically demanding, lyrically accountable — back when the mainstream wasn’t asking for it. Now that the tide is turning back toward craft, he’s already been there.

Six years. Eleven projects. No label, no team, no outside capital. Just an artist who decided that meaningful lyrics, honest self-examination, and technical precision still matter — and proved it a million streams later.

Why MXZY

Ottawa’s hip-hop scene has produced nationally recognized exports, but it remains underserved by consistent investment in the artists building it from the ground up. MXZY is one of a small number of Ottawa-based independent artists who has sustained a multi-year release schedule, built a real local live following, and earned outside recognition from touring acts and producers — all without institutional support.

Funding MXZY is an investment in an artist who has already proven he can build an audience, sell out his own shows, and hold his own on bills with nationally touring talent — with no team, no label, and no outside capital. With two new projects arriving in Fall 2026, the work is already done. What’s needed now is the infrastructure to match the output.

Press & Recognition

February 2024  ·  Arts & Entertainment

Featured in coverage of the Ottawa Black Creatives Hub Performing Arts Showcase at Commons Theatre, presented by the Algonquin College Student Association and Hors Pair Social for Black History Month. MXZY was profiled alongside other Black Ottawa artists and quoted on his approach to writing and the influence of Somali oral poetic tradition on his work.

“A lot of the shit I do is therapy rap. A lot of it comes from stuff I want to get out… People often don’t know this, but Somalia is known as a nation of poets.”
